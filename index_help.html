User Guide

  1. Create an account and login
  2. When on the internal home screen, enter a title in the text box -> click the make new file button.
  3. This will take you to the QuiNote text editor.
    • QuiNote understands your notes through placing of line breaks, colons, and semi-colons:
      • term 1: definition
      • term 2: definition 1; optional definition 2; optional definition 3
        • See below for a detailed syntax guide
    • You can also copy-paste in example notes if you want to play with making quizzes before playing with the editor. Here are a few in history of science, history of World Wars I and II, and psychology.
  4. To generate a quiz, enter what number of each type of question you'd like in the floating sidebar and click Generate Quiz

Syntax Guide

A complete "Note" is a term (or identifier, as we say), a separating colon and one or more definitions separated by semicolons:
Note: a term plus a definition; the fundamental element of QuiNote

Additionally, "aliases," or other names by which a Note can be known, can be added inside square brackets before the colon. Multiple aliases can be added with separating commas:
John F. Kennedy [JFK, John]: 1960s US president

Lists (just press the tab key) are collections of related elements, unified by their parent element. Lists can be recursively nested (press tab again).
Axis Powers in WWII:
  • Germany
    • Recursively nested
    • Facts about Germany
  • Japan
  • Italy
 Parts of a definition (the portion of a note that follows the colon) can be made into quote literals. Nothing inside the quotes will be parsed. In the example below, QuiNote would ignore the second colon:
Kurt Vonnegut: author of "Slaughterhouse-Five, or The Children's Crusade: A Duty Dance with Death"

Finally, you can write comments to yourself and indicate you don't want QuiNote to quiz you on them with parentheses:
(Final exam this Tuesday!)
